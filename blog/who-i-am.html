About Me Career

Who I Am

An introduction to my background, what excites me, and why I build things with data.

Hi, I'm Luyang Si. I'm a data analyst and researcher based in Champaign, Illinois, with a passion for turning raw data into actionable insights and solving real-world problems through rigorous analysis.

What I Do

I build the infrastructure that makes data useful. This means designing and maintaining data pipelines, translating complex datasets into business intelligence dashboards, and conducting research to understand how people behave and make decisions. Whether it's cleaning messy data, optimizing queries, or applying natural language processing to unstructured text, I enjoy the problem-solving aspect of working with data at every stage of the pipeline.

My work spans multiple domains:

  • Data Engineering: ETL pipelines, data quality frameworks, and robust SQL-based systems.
  • Business Intelligence: Power BI dashboards and KPI reporting that inform strategy and pricing decisions.
  • Behavioral Research: Designing experiments, analyzing causality, and measuring the impact of decisions on user behavior.
  • Applied NLP: From anomaly detection in scholarly metadata to analyzing patterns in text data.

How I Got Here

I grew up with a deep curiosity about how things work—not just the mechanics, but the "why" behind them. Data became my language for answering "why" questions at scale. During my graduate work, I realized that the most impactful projects weren't the ones that produced the most beautiful algorithm or the most sophisticated model—they were the ones that solved a real problem someone had.

That realization shaped my approach: I prioritize impact over novelty and clarity over complexity. I'd rather build a system that works reliably and scales, than build something cutting-edge that only I can maintain.

What Excites Me

A few things drive what I choose to work on:

1. Discovery through constraint: I love problems where you have imperfect data, contradictory signals, and limited resources—and you still need to make a decision. That's where critical thinking matters most.
2. Seeing the full pipeline: I want to understand both the "why" (research) and the "how" (engineering). Most of my favorite projects have involved moving between analysis and implementation.
3. Writing and explaining: Good analysis is only half the battle—you need to communicate it clearly. I care deeply about translating findings into language decision-makers actually understand.

Why This Blog

I'm starting this blog because I believe in learning in public. Over the years, I've accumulated approaches, tools, and lessons learned that might be useful to others working in similar spaces. This is a place to share insights from my projects, write about problems I've encountered and solved, and document the tools and practices that make data work more efficient.

I'm also just a writer at heart. Writing helps me think more clearly, organize complex ideas, and hold myself accountable to the quality of my work.

Let's Connect

If you're working on something interesting—whether it's a data pipeline, a research question, or just want to chat about building data systems—I'd love to hear from you. You can find me on GitHub, LinkedIn, or email.
